Frequently asked questions

What you need to know

Answers to the questions Bill hears most often before a first appointment.

How long is a typical session?
Sessions last 50 to 60 minutes and can be held in person or virtually via Google Meet.
How often can we meet?
Bill is extremely flexible. Some clients meet multiple times a week, others weekly, biweekly, or monthly. The goal is finding the frequency that works best for you.
What does a session typically look like?
Sessions vary in structure and scope. They consist of exploring thoughts, ideas, feelings, and experiences, along with setting goals and benchmarks to measure your growth.
Are you in-network with insurance?
Bill is not in-network with insurance companies. This preserves individualized care, clinical autonomy, your privacy, and allows him to work with clients on personal growth that may not meet a diagnosable threshold. HSA/FSA cards and all major credit cards are accepted.
How does payment work?
Bill accepts HSA/FSA cards and all major credit cards. Payments are processed through Square, which is PCI DSS compliant to keep your information secure.
Do you work with outside providers?
Yes. Bill collaborates with local psychiatrists, doctors, and school districts. He regularly attends IEP and 504 meetings to advocate on a client’s behalf and coordinates with psychiatrists when medication support is needed.
Why should athletes see a counselor for performance anxiety?
As a former coach, Bill understands that mental barriers are just as significant as physical ones. Counseling helps athletes identify triggers for “choking,” build resilience after injury, and develop a positive mindset that enhances focus both on and off the field.
What does ADHD counseling look like?
The approach focuses on practical strategies for executive functioning, time management, and emotional regulation. Sessions are action-oriented and build systems that work with your unique brain, whether you’re a student struggling with deadlines or an adult navigating workplace challenges.
Can you help navigate my child’s IEP or 504 plan?
Yes. Drawing on experience as a former school counselor, Bill helps families understand the intricacies of school-based supports, identify necessary accommodations, and request the right assessments. He has worked with families across over a dozen Chester County districts and private schools including Westtown, Salesianum, and The Tatnall School.
Do you help student-athletes with burnout and recruiting stress?
Absolutely. The pressure of being a student-athlete is immense. Bill helps students balance academics, intense practice schedules, and the emotional toll of the college recruiting process, keeping sports a source of joy rather than just another stressor.
